## Prompt Engineering Principles

### 1. Clear Scene Foundation

**Subject Definition:**
- Who/what is the primary focus?
- Physical characteristics and appearance
- Emotional state or energy
- Context and environment

**Example:**
```
Elderly Japanese woodworker in traditional workshop, weathered hands carefully shaping wood with hand plane, focused expression showing decades of mastery, surrounded by tools and natural wood pieces
```

### 2. Style Specification

**Visual Aesthetics:**
- Photorealistic vs Stylized
- Clean vs Textured
- Modern vs Vintage
- Commercial vs Artistic

**Color Grading:**
- Warm/Cool tones
- Saturation levels
- Contrast preferences
- Specific color palettes

**Example:**
```
Style: Warm cinematic realism with slight vintage film aesthetic, desaturated slightly, soft grain texture, earthy color palette emphasizing natural wood tones and warm skin tones
```

### 3. Motion Control

**Camera Motion:**
- Static (locked-off)
- Dolly (in/out)
- Pan (left/right)
- Tilt (up/down)
- Orbit (around subject)
- Tracking (following)
- Handheld (natural shake)

**Motion Speed:**
- Very Slow: Contemplative, dreamlike
- Slow: Cinematic, professional
- Medium: Natural, documentary
- Fast: Energetic, dynamic
- Very Fast: Action, excitement

**Subject Motion:**
- Minimal: Subtle movements only
- Moderate: Natural actions
- High: Dynamic action sequences

**Example:**
```
Camera: Slow dolly forward at 0.5 fps, smooth stabilized
Subject: Gentle deliberate movements, wood shavings falling slowly
Motion Intensity: Low (peaceful, meditative pace)
```

### 4. Quality Settings

**Resolution/Quality Tiers:**
- Standard: Fast generation, good for previews
- High: Balanced quality and speed
- Premium: Maximum quality, longer processing

**Detail Level:**
- Soft focus, dreamy
- Standard sharpness
- High detail, crisp
- Ultra-detailed, textured

### 5. Duration Optimization

**Short (3-6 seconds):**
- Single focused action
- Quick moments
- Transitions

**Medium (7-12 seconds):**
- Complete action sequences
- Scene establishment
- Story beats

**Long (13-20+ seconds):**
- Extended sequences
- Multiple actions
- Narrative development

## Motion Intensity Guidelines

### Low Intensity (Smooth, Minimal)
**Use for:**
- Product showcases
- Contemplative scenes
- ASMR-style content
- Luxury/premium feel

**Characteristics:**
- Slow deliberate movements
- Smooth stabilized camera
- Gentle subject actions
- Peaceful energy

**Example:**
```
Motion Intensity: Low
Camera: Ultra-slow dolly orbit around subject at 0.3 fps
Subject: Minimal movement, gentle rotation only
Energy: Calm, meditative, luxurious
```

### Medium Intensity (Natural, Balanced)
**Use for:**
- Lifestyle content
- Documentary style
- Story-driven scenes
- General content

**Characteristics:**
- Natural paced movements
- Realistic actions
- Balanced energy
- Authentic feel

**Example:**
```
Motion Intensity: Medium
Camera: Steady tracking following subject naturally
Subject: Normal walking pace, natural gestures
Energy: Authentic, relatable, engaging
```

### High Intensity (Dynamic, Energetic)
**Use for:**
- Sports/action
- Music videos
- High-energy content
- Exciting moments

**Characteristics:**
- Fast movements

- Quick actions
- Vibrant energy

**Example:**
```

Subject: Rapid athletic movements, jumping, spinning
Energy: Explosive, exciting, adrenaline-filled
```

## Best Practices

✅ **DO:**
- Be specific about desired motion type and speed
- Specify style clearly upfront
- Include lighting and atmosphere details
- Define quality expectations
- Describe intended mood/energy
- Reference successful visual styles
- Provide clear subject descriptions

❌ **AVOID:**
- Overly complex multi-action sequences
- Contradictory style directions
- Vague motion descriptions
- Unrealistic physics expectations
- Excessive technical jargon
- Too many simultaneous elements
